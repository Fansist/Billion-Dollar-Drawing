      Questions you're too polite to ask

      Why is it called the Billion Dollar Drawing if it maxes out at one million?

      Branding. If we sell every single pixel, we raise $1,000,000 — which is exactly 0.1% of a billion. We considered renaming it the "0.1% of a Billion Dollar Drawing," but it didn't fit on the hat.

      Is this a real investment?

      No. It is a parody. The only thing appreciating here is the joke. Please do not remortgage anything.

      Do I actually pay money?

      No real payment is taken. Your masterpiece is saved in your own browser using localStorage, which is the digital equivalent of writing your name on a napkin and keeping it.

      What if I buy a single pixel?

      Then for one cent you own a square so small that on most screens it is a rumor. Bold. We respect it.

      Can I draw something inappropriate?

      It's your browser, your napkin. But the imaginary moderators of this imaginary economy are watching with imaginary disappointment.

      Will this make me famous?

      Among people who zoom into a 10,000×10,000 grid looking for your specific 4 pixels? Absolutely. All three of them.
